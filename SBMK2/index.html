Plain ol' muffins
An easy recipe for reliable, fluffy muffins.
Universal
23/04/2026
  • Prep Time
    Cook Time
    Servings
    15 Minutes
    25 Minutes
    12 Standard-sized muffins
  • Ingredientes
    2 cups
    All-purpose flour
    ½ cup
    Sugar
    1 tbsp
    Baking powder
    ½ tsp
    Salt
    1 cup
    Milk
    1
    Large Egg
    ¼ cup
    Vegetable oil (or melted butter)
  • Procedure
Easily upgradable by tossing in a handful of chocolate chips or blueberries to the dry mix in Step 2
  1. Prep: Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F) and grease a 12-cup muffin tin.
  2. Dry Mix: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Wet Mix: In a separate smaller bowl, beat the egg, then stir in the milk and oil.
  4. Combine: Pour the wet ingredients into the dry. Stir gently until just combined—lumps are fine; overmixing makes them tough.
  5. Bake: Divide the batter into the tin and bake for 18–20 minutes until golden and a toothpick comes out clean.
Simply delicious Brownie
Whip up this easy, savory gnocchi in no time. Simply crisp the bacon, boil the gnocchi, and toss together for perfection.
Italian Cuisine
23/04/2026
  • This recipe focuses on achieving that perfect contrast between pillowy soft gnocchi and the salty, smoky crunch of bacon. It is designed to be efficient without sacrificing that "from-scratch" quality.
  • Prep Time
    Cook Time
    Servings
    15 Minutes
    10 Minutes
    4 People
  • Ingredientes
    500g
    Fresh potato gnocchi
    200g
    Thick-cut smoked bacon, diced
    2 cloves
    Garlic, minced
    1/2 cup
    Heavy cream (or a splash of pasta water for a lighter version)
    1/2 cup
    Freshly grated Parmesan cheese
    1 tbsp
    Extra virgin olive oil
    Handful
    Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Procedure
youFeel free to make any changes to adapt it to your needs
  1. Boil the Water: Fill a large pot with water, add a generous pinch of salt, and bring it to a rolling boil.
  2. Prep the Bacon: While the water heats, dice your bacon into uniform 1cm pieces to ensure even cooking.
  3. Cold Pan Start: Place the bacon in a large skillet over medium heat before the pan gets hot. This helps render the fat for maximum crunch.
  4. Sauté to Gold: Fry the bacon until it is deeply golden and crispy. Use a slotted spoon to remove the bacon bits and set them aside on a paper towel.
  5. Manage the Fat: Leave about 1 tablespoon of the rendered bacon fat in the skillet; discard the rest or save it for later.
  6. Aromatize: Add the olive oil and minced garlic to the skillet. Sauté for 30–60 seconds until fragrant but not browned.
  7. Cook the Gnocchi: Drop the gnocchi into the boiling water. They are ready the moment they float to the surface (usually 2–3 minutes).
  8. Emulsify: Before draining, scoop out 1/2 cup of starchy pasta water and set it aside.
  9. Combine: Transfer the drained gnocchi directly into the skillet with the garlic and oil.
  10. The Sear: Increase the heat to medium-high and let the gnocchi sear for 1 minute without moving them to get a slight crust.
  11. Sauce Base: Pour in the heavy cream (or the reserved pasta water) and the grated Parmesan cheese.
  12. The Toss: Stir gently but constantly until the cheese melts and creates a glossy, velvety coating over the gnocchi.
  13. Final Integration: Fold the crispy bacon bits back into the pan so they stay crunchy.
  14. Season: Add a healthy crack of black pepper. (You likely won't need salt due to the bacon and Parmesan).
  15. Garnish & Serve: Remove from heat, sprinkle with fresh parsley, and serve immediately while the steam is still rising.
