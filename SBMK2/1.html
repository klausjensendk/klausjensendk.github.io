Chocolate Chip Muffins Recipe
  • Ingredients for 12 Muffins
  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • ⅓ cup vegetable oil (or melted butter)
  • 1 large egg
  • ⅓–½ cup milk (adjust for batter consistency)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips

1. Preheat oven: to 200°C (400°F). Line a 12-cup muffin tin with paper liners.

2. Mix dry ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, salt, and baking powder.

3. Prepare wet mixture: In a separate bowl, whisk oil, egg, milk, and vanilla until smooth.

4. Combine: Pour wet mixture into dry ingredients. Stir gently until just combined (avoid overmixing).

5. Add chocolate chips: Fold in chocolate chips evenly.

6. Fill muffin cups: Divide batter among liners, filling about ¾ full. Sprinkle extra chips on top if desired.

7. Bake: Place in oven for 18–22 minutes, until golden and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.

8. Cool & enjoy: Let muffins rest in the pan for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack.

Tip: For richer flavor, replace half the flour with cocoa powder to make double chocolate muffins.

Lemon Ricotta Pasta (Splendid Dish)
  • Ingredients for 4 Portions
  • 350 - 400 g Pasta (Spaghetti, Linguine, or Rigatoni)
  • 250 g (1 cup) Ricotta Cheese (whole milk)
  • ½ cup Parmesan Cheese (grated, plus more for serving)
  • 1 large Lemon (zest and juice)
  • 3 cloves Garlic (minced)
  • ½ cup Fresh Basil (chopped, or parsley)
  • 4 Tablespoons Olive Oil (Extra Virgin)
  • ½ teaspoon Red Pepper Flakes (optional)

1. Boil Pasta: Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil and **salt it heavily**. Add pasta and cook until *al dente* (firm to the bite). **Reserve 1 to 1½ cups of the starchy water** before draining.

2. Prepare Ricotta Sauce: In a very large bowl, combine the ricotta, ½ cup grated Parmesan, lemon zest, lemon juice, and red pepper flakes (if using). Whisk until smooth. **Do not add salt yet.**

3. Make Garlic Oil: In a small skillet, gently heat the 4 Tbsps of olive oil over medium-low heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for **1–2 minutes** until fragrant. **Do not let the garlic brown.** Remove from heat.

4. Combine & Emulsify: Add the hot, drained pasta and the garlic-infused olive oil (garlic and all) directly to the bowl with the ricotta mixture. Begin tossing vigorously.

5. Adjust Consistency: Slowly ladle in the reserved hot pasta water, a splash at a time, while continuing to toss. The heat and starch will create a creamy, emulsified sauce. Use just enough water to reach your desired consistency.

6. Season & Garnish: Season generously with salt and freshly ground black pepper. Stir in half of the fresh basil/parsley. Divide into four bowls and top with the remaining herbs and extra Parmesan.

Tip: For the best flavor and texture, make sure the pasta water is very hot when you add it to the ricotta mixture.

  • Enjoy!
