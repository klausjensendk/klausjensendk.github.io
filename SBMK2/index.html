Gnocchi with Bacon à la Marta
Whip up this easy, savory gnocchi in no time. Simply crisp the bacon, boil the pillows, and toss together for perfection.
Italian Cuisine
23/04/2026
  • This recipe focuses on achieving that perfect contrast between pillowy soft gnocchi and the salty, smoky crunch of bacon. It is designed to be efficient without sacrificing that "from-scratch" quality.
  • Prep Time
    Cook Time
    Servings
    15 Minutes
    10 Minutes
    4 People
  • Ingredientes
    500g
    Fresh potato gnocchi
    200g
    Thick-cut smoked bacon, diced
    2 cloves
    Garlic, minced
    1/2 cup
    Heavy cream (or a splash of pasta water for a lighter version)
    1/2 cup
    Freshly grated Parmesan cheese
    1 tbsp
    Extra virgin olive oil
    Handful
    Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Procedure
youFeel free to make any changes to adadt it to your needs
  1. Boil the Water: Fill a large pot with water, add a generous pinch of salt, and bring it to a rolling boil.
  2. Prep the Bacon: While the water heats, dice your bacon into uniform 1cm pieces to ensure even cooking.

  4. Cold Pan Start: Place the bacon in a large skillet over medium heat before the pan gets hot. This helps render the fat for maximum crunch.
  5. Sauté to Gold: Fry the bacon until it is deeply golden and crispy. Use a slotted spoon to remove the bacon bits and set them aside on a paper towel.
  6. Manage the Fat: Leave about 1 tablespoon of the rendered bacon fat in the skillet; discard the rest or save it for later.
  7. Aromatize: Add the olive oil and minced garlic to the skillet. Sauté for 30–60 seconds until fragrant but not browned.
  8. Cook the Gnocchi: Drop the gnocchi into the boiling water. They are ready the moment they float to the surface (usually 2–3 minutes).
  9. Emulsify: Before draining, scoop out 1/2 cup of starchy pasta water and set it aside.
  10. Combine: Transfer the drained gnocchi directly into the skillet with the garlic and oil.
  11. The Sear: Increase the heat to medium-high and let the gnocchi sear for 1 minute without moving them to get a slight crust.
  12. Sauce Base: Pour in the heavy cream (or the reserved pasta water) and the grated Parmesan cheese.
  13. The Toss: Stir gently but constantly until the cheese melts and creates a glossy, velvety coating over the gnocchi.
  14. Final Integration: Fold the crispy bacon bits back into the pan so they stay crunchy.
  15. Season: Add a healthy crack of black pepper. (You likely won't need salt due to the bacon and Parmesan).
  16. Garnish & Serve: Remove from heat, sprinkle with fresh parsley, and serve immediately while the steam is still rising.
