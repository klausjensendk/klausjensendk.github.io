Flæskesteg med rødkål
Ingredients
For the Flæskesteg (Roast Pork)
  • Pork Roast: 1.5 - 2 kg, rind on, scored
  • Coarse Sea Salt: 1-2 tbsp
  • Bay Leaves: 5-6 whole leaves
  • Whole Cloves: 10-12 (optional)
  • Water: ~500 ml for the pan
For the Rødkål (Red Cabbage)
  • Red Cabbage: 1 medium head (~1 kg), shredded
  • Fat: 2 tbsp butter or duck fat
  • Redcurrant Juice: 150-200 ml
  • Apple Cider Vinegar: 100 ml
Method
Preparing the Flæskesteg
  1. Preheat & Prep: Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F). Ensure the pork rind is deeply scored, cutting just to the meat. If not, carefully score it with a sharp knife. Pat the rind completely dry with paper towels—this is crucial for crispy crackling.
  2. Season Generously: Vigorously rub the coarse salt into the scores and all over the rind. This draws out moisture and creates the bubbles for the crackling. Press the bay leaves and optional cloves down into the scores.
  3. Slow Roast: Reduce oven temperature to 160°C (320°F). Continue to roast for 1.5 - 2 hours, or until the internal temperature reaches 65°C (150°F). If the crackling isn't perfect, you can give it a final blast under the grill for 2-5 minutes at the end—watch it carefully to prevent burning!
  4. Rest the Meat: Remove from the oven and let it rest for at least 15-20 minutes before carving. This ensures a juicy, tender roast.
